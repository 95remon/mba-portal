--- Slide 12 ---
Returns to Scale
If inputs are increased, by how much will output increase?
Increasing returns to scale
 – output goes up by more than increase in inputs
Decreasing returns to scale
 – output goes up by less than increase in inputs
Constant returns to scale
 – same increase

--- Slide 13 ---
Long-Run Average Cost
Long-run average cost
(LAC)
Measures cost per unit of output when optimal amount of each input is used
LAC
is 
U-shaped
Falling 
LAC
 indicates 
economies of scale
Rising 
LAC
 indicates 
diseconomies of scale

--- Slide 14 ---
Long-Run Marginal Cost
Long-run marginal cost
(LMC)
Measures rate of change in LR total cost as output changes along expansion path
LMC
 is 
U-shaped
LMC
 lies below 
LAC
 when 
LAC
 is falling
LMC
 lies above 
LAC
 when 
LAC
 is rising
LMC = LAC
 at the minimum value of 
LAC

Diseconomies of scale

--- Slide 18 ---
Economies of Scope
Economies of Scope
Exist when joint cost of producing two or more goods is less than the sum of the separate costs of producing the goods
For two goods, 
X
 &amp; 
Y
, economies of scope exist when:
C(X, Y) &lt; C(X) + C(Y)
