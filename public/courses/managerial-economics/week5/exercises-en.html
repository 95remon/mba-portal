Question 1

The total cost schedule for a competitive firm is given below. Fixed costs are $60.

Output (Q) Variable Cost (VC)
0 0
1 10
2 15
3 30
4 60
5 105

Questions:

  1. If the market price is $15, how many units should the firm produce?
  2. What is the maximum profit that the firm can earn?
  3. What is the break-even price for the firm?
  4. What is the shut-down price for the firm?

Explanation

To solve this, we must first calculate Total Cost (TC), Marginal Cost (MC), Average Total Cost (ATC), and Average Variable Cost (AVC) for each output level.

  • $\text{TC} = \text{FC} + \text{VC}$
  • $\text{MC} = \frac{\Delta \text{TC}}{\Delta \text{Q}}$
  • $\text{ATC} = \frac{\text{TC}}{\text{Q}}$
  • $\text{AVC} = \frac{\text{VC}}{\text{Q}}$

Completed Cost Schedule:

Q VC FC TC MC ATC AVC
0 0 60 60 --- --- ---
1 10 60 70 10 70 10
2 15 60 75 5 37.5 7.5
3 30 60 90 15 30 10
4 60 60 120 30 30 15
5 105 60 165 45 33 21

1) Optimal Output

A perfectly competitive firm maximizes profit by producing where Marginal Revenue equals Marginal Cost ($MR = MC$). Since the firm is a price taker, $P = MR = 15$. Looking at the schedule:

  • At Q = 3, $MC = 15$. Therefore, the firm should produce 3.0 units. (Note: Choose Q such that MR >= MC before MC exceeds MR).

2) Maximum Profit

Profit is calculated as Total Revenue minus Total Cost: $\text{Profit} = \text{TR} - \text{TC} = (P \times Q) - \text{TC}$

  • $\text{TR} = \$15 \times 3 = \$45$
  • $\text{TC}$ at Q=3 is $\$90$.
  • $\text{Profit} = 45 - 90 = \mathbf{-\$45}$. The firm incurs a loss of $45, but this minimizes total losses compared to shutting down (which incurs a $60 fixed cost loss).

3) Break-Even Price

The Break-even Price is the absolute minimum value on the Average Total Cost (ATC) curve. From the table, the lowest ATC is $30 (occurring between Q=3 and Q=4). Therefore, the Break-Even Price is $30.

4) Shut-Down Price

The Shut-down Price is the absolute minimum value on the Average Variable Cost (AVC) curve. From the table, the lowest AVC is $7.5 (occurring at Q=2). Therefore, the Shut-down Price is $7.5.

Question 2

The graph below depicts the cost curves for a perfectly competitive firm.

Questions:

  1. If market price is $5, how much output should the firm produce?
  2. If market price is $5, how much profit will the firm earn?
  3. If market price is $3, how much profit will the firm earn?
  4. What is the expected long-run price in this industry?

Explanation

1) Optimal Output at P = $5

The profit-maximizing rule dictates choosing Q such that $MR (= P) = MC$. Locating $P = 5$ on the y-axis and tracing to the Marginal Cost (MC) curve yields an output of 600 units.

2) Profit at P = $5

Profit can be calculated using per-unit margins: $\text{Profit} = (P - \text{ATC}) \times Q$ At Q = 600:

  • $P = \$5$
  • $ATC = \$4$ (from the graph)
  • $\text{Profit} = (\$5 - \$4) \times 600 = \mathbf{\$600}$.

3) Profit at P = $3

First, determine optimal output where $P = MC$. At $P = 3$, $MC$ intersects at Q = 400. At Q = 400:

  • $P = \$3$
  • $ATC = \$4$
  • $\text{Profit} = (\$3 - \$4) \times 400 = \mathbf{-\$400}$. The firm earns a negative profit (loss) of $400.

4) Expected Long-Run Price

In the long run, free entry and exit force economic profits to zero. This occurs at the Break-even Price, which is the intersection of MC and the minimum of the ATC curve. Observing the minimum of the ATC curve, $MC = ATC$ at approximately $3.9.

Question 3

The following graph presents a perfectly competitive firm utilizing a single variable input (labor).

Questions:

  1. If the wage is $20, how many workers will the firm hire?
  2. If the wage is $15, how many workers will the firm hire?
  3. At what wage will the firm shut down and hire 0 workers in the short run?

Explanation

1) Workers Hired at Wage = $20

A profit-maximizing firm hires labor up to the point where the Wage rate equals the Marginal Revenue Product ($W = \text{MRP}$). Tracing the wage of $20 to the MRP curve reveals the firm will hire 175 units of labor.

2) Workers Hired at Wage = $15

Repeating the optimal hiring rule ($W = \text{MRP}$), tracing $15 to the MRP curve dictates the firm will hire 200 units of labor.

3) Shut-Down Wage

The firm will cease hiring completely in the short run when the wage exceeds the peak of the Average Revenue Product (ARP) curve. This critical threshold is identified where $MRP = ARP$. The intersection of MRP and ARP occurs at a wage of $34.0. Above this wage, the firm shuts down.
